About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863â€“1902) was a Hindu monk and a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He played a major role in the revival of Hinduism in India and contributed to the concept of nationalism in colonial India.

His teachings emphasized the unity of all religions and the importance of service to humanity. Swami Vivekananda's philosophy and thoughts continue to inspire millions around the globe.

Thoughts and Teachings

"Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."

Swami Vivekananda's teachings focused on self-realization, self-confidence, and the power of the mind. He believed in the potential of every individual to realize their true self and contribute positively to society.

"The greatest religion is to be true to your own nature. Have faith in yourselves!"

He advocated for the importance of education that enables individuals to think critically and act with compassion towards others.
